            Samuel Glasgow Dickson
            (born 1947) is a prominent
            [Atlanta](/wiki/Atlanta)
            attorney and
            [Southern](/wiki/The_South)
            historian who has been active in
            [White nationalist](/wiki/White_nationalist)
            causes. Dickson became politically and racially aware at a young age. At age 14, he protested the integration of his Presbyterian Church and by 16 was active in the Senator
            [Barry Goldwater](/wiki/Barry_Goldwater)
            presidential campaign of 1964.
          He attended the University of Georgia in the late 1960s and was active in the conservative organizations Young Republicans and Young Americans for Freedom. He graduated from the  University of Georgia Law School in 1972 and opened his law offices in Atlanta. He offers legal counsel to White activists and in 1978 ran for Lieutenant Governor of
            [Georgia](/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state))
            as a
            [segregationist](/wiki/Segregationist)
            against Zell Miller
            winning 11% of the vote. While running, he was barred from taking out advertisement space from the Atlanta Journal-Constitution.
          Sam Dickson is a member of the
            [Council of Conservative Citizens](/wiki/Council_of_Conservative_Citizens)
            and has given lectures at the annual conference of
            [American Renaissance](/wiki/American_Renaissance)
            . In college he was a member of the
            [Union of the American People](/wiki/Union_of_the_American_People)
            .
