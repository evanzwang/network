About Andrew Anh Tuan Tran

Background

Andrew A. Tran, M.D. is an orthopaedic surgeon with a fellowship sub-specialization in sports medicine. He obtained his undergraduate degree in biochemistry at Brown University and his medical degree from Stanford University. He completed his residency training at the George Washington University and a sports medicine fellowship at the Cleveland Clinic, where he was a team physician at the professional and collegiate level.

Dr. Tran manages all general orthopaedic conditions of the upper and lower extremity. In addition to general orthopaedic surgery, he specializes in complex surgery, arthroscopy, reconstruction, and joint preservation of the shoulder, elbow, hip, and knee.

Beyond his clinical work, Dr. Tran has published over 30 peer-reviewed publications and is a member of the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons and the American Orthopaedic Society for Sports Medicine.
